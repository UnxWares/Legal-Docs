## 2. General principles

UnxWares is committed to processing personal data in compliance with the following fundamental principles:

### 2.1. Lawfulness, fairness and transparency

Data is collected and processed lawfully, fairly and transparently. Users are informed of the collection and use of their data.

### 2.2. Purpose limitation

Data is collected for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes, and not further processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes.

### 2.3. Data minimization

Only adequate, relevant and limited data necessary for the purposes are collected.

### 2.4. Accuracy

Data is accurate and kept up to date. All reasonable steps are taken to ensure that inaccurate data is erased or rectified.

### 2.5. Storage limitation

Data is kept for a period not exceeding that necessary for the purposes for which it is processed.

### 2.6. Integrity and confidentiality

Data is processed in a manner that ensures appropriate security, including protection against destruction, loss, alteration or unauthorized disclosure.

### 2.7. Accountability

UnxWares is able to demonstrate compliance of its processing with GDPR principles.

## 6. Data transfers outside the European Union

### 6.1. Limitation principle

UnxWares strives to limit as much as possible the transfer of personal data outside the European Union and the European Economic Area (EEA).

### 6.2. Necessary transfers

Some subcontractors may be located outside the EU/EEA:

**Note on Switzerland:**
Switzerland benefits from an adequacy decision from the European Commission dated July 26, 2000 (amended in 2023) recognizing a level of personal data protection essentially equivalent to that of the EU. Transfers to Switzerland are therefore facilitated, provided that the Swiss recipient does not subsequently transfer the data to a third country without appropriate safeguards.

### 6.3. Protection guarantees

When a transfer outside the EU/EEA is necessary, UnxWares ensures that appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with GDPR:

**Mechanisms used:**

- **Adequacy decision** from the European Commission (if the country has an adequate level of protection)
- **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCC)** approved by the European Commission
- **Binding Corporate Rules (BCR)** for international groups
- **Certifications** (Privacy Shield for the United States before its invalidation, replaced by the Data Privacy Framework if applicable)

**Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA):**
In accordance with the recommendations of the European Data Protection Board (EDPB), UnxWares assesses the risks associated with each transfer and implements additional protective measures if necessary.

### 6.4. User information

## 7. Data retention period

UnxWares only retains personal data for the period strictly necessary for the purposes for which it is processed.

### 7.1. Active account data

**Period:** During the entire duration of the contractual relationship (active account with subscribed services).

**Data concerned:** All account data (identification, contact, technical, etc.).

### 7.2. Data after termination or account closure

**Period:** 3 years from the end of the contractual relationship (termination, account deletion).

**Purpose:** Commercial prescription (Article L110-4 of the Commercial Code), management of possible complaints, proof in case of litigation.

**Data concerned:** Identification data, contact data, minimal contractual history.

**Modality:** Intermediate archiving with restricted access.

### 7.3. Billing data

**Period:** 10 years from the invoice date.

**Purpose:** Legal accounting and tax obligation (Article L123-22 of the Commercial Code, Tax Procedures Book).

**Data concerned:** Invoices, billing details, amounts, dates, references.

**Modality:** Secure archiving with strictly limited access.

### 7.4. Connection data (logs)

**Period:** 12 months from the connection date.

**Purpose:** Legal obligation (Article 6-II of the Law for Confidence in the Digital Economy - LCEN).

**Data concerned:** Connection logs (IP address, date, time, identifier).

**Modality:** Secure archiving with access reserved for security teams and legally authorized authorities.

### 7.5. Technical support data

**Period:** 3 years from ticket closure or last interaction.

**Purpose:** Service improvement, complaint management, proof in case of litigation.

**Data concerned:** Exchange history, tickets, attachments.

**Modality:** Archiving with restricted access to the support team.

### 7.6. Fraud and abuse data

**Period:** Up to 5 years after fraud or abuse detection.

**Purpose:** Recidivism prevention, infrastructure and other customer protection, proof in case of judicial proceedings.

**Data concerned:** Data related to detected fraud or abuse incidents.

**Modality:** Secure archiving with strictly limited access to management and security team.

### 7.7. Definitive deletion

At the end of the planned retention periods, personal data is either:

- **Permanently deleted** irreversibly
- **Anonymized** irreversibly (no longer considered personal data)

Data contained in technical backups is deleted during the backup rotation cycle (generally within a maximum of 90 days after the end of retention).

## 8. Security and confidentiality

### 8.1. Security commitment

UnxWares attaches the highest importance to the security and confidentiality of personal data. Appropriate technical and organizational measures are implemented to protect data against:

- Accidental or unlawful destruction
- Accidental loss
- Alteration
- Unauthorized disclosure or access
- Any other form of unlawful processing

### 8.2. Technical security measures

**Encryption:**

- Communication encryption (SSL/TLS for all HTTPS connections)
- Password encryption (secure hashing with robust algorithms: bcrypt, Argon2)
- Encryption of sensitive data at rest (databases, backups)

**Access control:**

- Strong authentication for customer account access (possibility to enable two-factor authentication)
- Strict internal access control (least privilege principle)
- Logging and traceability of personal data access

**Infrastructure security:**

- Firewall and intrusion detection/prevention systems (IDS/IPS)
- Customer environment isolation (containerization, virtualization)
- Continuous monitoring 24/7
- Regular security updates and patch management
- Periodic penetration testing and security audits

**Backup and resilience:**

- Regular and redundant backups
- Business continuity and disaster recovery plans
- Geographic replication of critical data

### 8.3. Organizational security measures

**Training and awareness:**

- Regular employee training on data protection and security
- Awareness of phishing and social engineering risks
- Documented internal security procedures

**Incident management:**

- Security incident response procedure
- Dedicated incident management team (CSIRT)
- Rapid notification in case of data breach (see article 8.5)

**Security policy:**

- Formalized information systems security policy (ISSP)
- Regular review of policies and procedures
- Compliance with standards and best practices (ISO 27001 in progress if applicable)

### 8.4. Confidentiality

All UnxWares employees with access to personal data are subject to a **strict confidentiality obligation**, contractually formalized.

Subcontractors are also bound by confidentiality clauses.

### 8.5. Data breach notification

In the event of a personal data breach likely to pose a risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects, UnxWares commits to:

**Notification to supervisory authority:**

- Within **72 hours** of discovering the breach (Article 33 of GDPR)
- Documentation of the breach (nature, data concerned, consequences, measures taken)

**Notification to data subjects:**

- Without undue delay if the breach is likely to result in a high risk (Article 34 of GDPR)
- Clear and transparent communication on the nature of the breach and measures to take

**Corrective measures:**

- Immediate implementation of measures to limit the breach's impact
- Root cause analysis and security strengthening to prevent recurrence

### 8.6. Security limitations

Despite all efforts by UnxWares, **no system is completely infallible**. UnxWares cannot guarantee absolute security against all forms of attack or vulnerability.

Users are also responsible for the security of their own systems, credentials and access devices.

## 9. Rights of data subjects

In accordance with GDPR, any person whose personal data is processed by UnxWares has the following rights:

### 9.1. Right of access (Article 15 of GDPR)

You have the right to obtain:

- Confirmation whether data concerning you is being processed
- Access to your personal data
- Information about processing (purposes, data categories, recipients, retention period, etc.)
- A copy of your personal data (the first copy is free)

### 9.2. Right of rectification (Article 16 of GDPR)

You have the right to have inaccurate or incomplete data concerning you rectified, without undue delay.

You can update most of your data directly from your customer area.

### 9.3. Right to erasure / "Right to be forgotten" (Article 17 of GDPR)

You have the right to obtain the erasure of your personal data in the following cases:

- The data is no longer necessary for the purposes
- You withdraw your consent (if processing was based on consent)
- You object to processing and there is no overriding legitimate reason
- The data has been unlawfully processed
- The data must be erased to comply with a legal obligation

**Limits:** This right does not apply when processing is necessary:

- For compliance with a legal obligation (e.g., invoice retention 10 years)
- For the establishment, exercise or defense of legal claims
- For reasons of public interest

### 9.4. Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 of GDPR)

You have the right to obtain restriction of processing of your data in the following cases:

- You contest the accuracy of the data (during verification period)
- Processing is unlawful and you prefer restriction to erasure
- UnxWares no longer needs the data but you need it for a legal claim
- You have objected to processing (pending verification of legitimate grounds)

**Effect:** Data is kept but can no longer be processed (except legal exceptions).

### 9.5. Right to data portability (Article 20 of GDPR)

You have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format, and to transmit it to another controller.

**Conditions:**

- Processing is based on consent or a contract
- Processing is carried out by automated means

**Data concerned:** Mainly your identification data, contact data, and configuration data (excluding logs and system-generated data).

### 9.6. Right to object (Article 21 of GDPR)

You have the right to object at any time, on grounds relating to your particular situation, to processing of your data based on UnxWares' legitimate interest.

**Special cases:**

- **Direct marketing**: absolute right to object without justification (if this feature is activated)
- **Other processing**: UnxWares can no longer process the data, except overriding legitimate grounds or defense of legal claims

### 9.7. Right to withdraw consent

When processing is based on your consent (e.g., marketing communications if activated), you have the right to withdraw this consent at any time, without justification.

Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

### 9.8. Right to define post-mortem directives (Article 85 of the Data Protection Act)

You have the right to define directives relating to the retention, erasure and communication of your personal data after your death.

These directives can be:

- **General**: registered with a trusted digital third party certified by the supervisory authority
- **Specific**: concerning specifically the data processed by UnxWares, communicated directly to UnxWares

**Exercise procedures:**

You can exercise your rights by contacting the Data Protection Officer:

**Analytics and performance cookies (subject to consent):**

- For the moment, **UnxWares does not use analytics or marketing tracking cookies**.
- If this feature is implemented in the future, your prior consent will be obtained.

**Advertising and social network cookies:**

- **UnxWares does not use advertising cookies** or social network cookies for the moment.

### 10.3. Third-party cookies

Certain cookies may be placed by third-party services:

- **Stripe**: for secure payment processing (necessary cookies)

These cookies are subject to the privacy policies of these third parties.

- Delete already saved cookies
